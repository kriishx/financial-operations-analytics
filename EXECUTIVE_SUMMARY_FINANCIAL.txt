SECTION 1 - REVENUE METRICS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

- Total All-Time Revenue earned from all customers since the start is 35,834,169 dollars
- Current Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) meaning how much money comes in every month is 1,160,806 dollars
- Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) meaning the yearly revenue estimate based on current MRR is 13,929,672 dollars
- Average Revenue Per Account meaning how much each customer brings in on average is 7,167 dollars
- Revenue has grown by 13.26 percent over the last 6 months which shows strong and consistent growth
- The monthly revenue trend from 2020 to 2024 shows a very clean and steady upward line with almost no sudden drops
- Revenue started near zero in early 2020 and has reached over 1.1 million dollars per month by late 2024

SECTION 2 - CUSTOMER METRICS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

- Total number of customers in the dataset is 5,000
- Active customers who are currently using the product is 4,344 which is 86.88 percent of total
- Churned customers meaning customers who left the product is 656 which is 13.12 percent of total
- Overall churn rate is 13.12 percent which means roughly 1 in every 8 customers eventually leaves
- Average customer lifetime meaning how long a customer stays before leaving is 26.9 months which is about 2.25 years
- Customers who are still active have an average lifetime of around 45 months shown in the box plot
- Customers who churned had an average lifetime of only about 12 to 13 months showing early dropout

CUSTOMER DISTRIBUTION BY SEGMENT

- Small Business segment has the most customers at 1,993 which is 39.9 percent of total
- Mid-Market segment has 1,258 customers which is 25.2 percent of total
- Startup segment has 1,236 customers which is 24.7 percent of total
- Enterprise segment has the fewest customers at 513 which is only 10.3 percent of total

CHURN RATE BY SEGMENT

- Mid-Market has the highest churn rate at 13.7 percent
- Enterprise has a churn rate of 13.3 percent
- Startup has a churn rate of 13.2 percent
- Small Business has the lowest churn rate at 12.7 percent
- The overall average churn rate across all segments is 13.2 percent
- Churn rates are very similar across all segments meaning no single segment is much better or worse at retaining customers

CHURN BY INDUSTRY

- Finance industry has the highest churn rate at 14.0 percent
- Technology comes second at 13.6 percent
- Healthcare is at 13.5 percent
- Education has one of the lower churn rates at 12.1 percent
- The Other category has the lowest churn rate at 9.6 percent

CHURN BY CONTRACT LENGTH

- Customers on very short contracts have the highest churn at around 13.9 percent
- As contract length increases churn drops significantly
- Customers on 25-month contracts have the lowest churn at 11.9 percent
- Customers on 36-month contracts have a churn rate of 12.5 percent
- Key Insight - Longer contracts lead to better customer retention

USAGE SCORE OBSERVATION

- Active customers have an average usage score of 66.0
- Churned customers have a lower average usage score of 54.7
- This clearly shows that customers who use the product more tend to stay longer
- Key Insight - Low usage is a warning sign that a customer might leave soon

SECTION 3 - CUSTOMER VALUE METRICS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

- Average Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) is 7,167 dollars meaning that is the average total money a customer brings in
- Median CLV is 2,907 dollars which is much lower than the mean showing that a few high-value customers pull the average up
- CLV to Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) Ratio is 14.33 times assuming a CAC of 500 dollars
- A CLV to CAC ratio above 3 is generally considered very healthy so 14.33 is an excellent result
- Average Payback Period meaning how long it takes to recover the cost of acquiring a customer is only 5.0 months
- A short payback period of 5 months means the business recovers its investment quickly

CLV BY SEGMENT

- Mid-Market customers have the highest average CLV at 7,478 dollars
- Small Business customers have average CLV of 7,099 dollars
- Startup customers have average CLV of 7,025 dollars
- Enterprise customers have the lowest average CLV at 7,011 dollars
- Key Insight - CLV is fairly similar across all segments with Mid-Market slightly ahead

CLV TREND BY COHORT YEAR

- Customers who joined in 2020 have the highest CLV at around 11,900 dollars because they have been with the company the longest
- CLV drops for later cohorts simply because newer customers have had less time to generate revenue
- This is normal and expected in subscription businesses

SECTION 4 - REVENUE FORECASTING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

- Two forecasting models were used to predict future revenue - ARIMA and Facebook Prophet
- Both models agree closely on future revenue predictions showing the forecast is reliable
- Forecasted Revenue for the next 12 months is 15,710,149 dollars
- Expected average monthly revenue over the next 12 months is 1,309,179 dollars
- Current MRR is 1,160,806 dollars so this represents a further increase in monthly revenue
- Model Accuracy measured by MAPE (Mean Absolute Percentage Error) is only 0.73 percent
- A MAPE below 5 percent is considered excellent so 0.73 percent shows very high forecasting accuracy
- ARIMA model shows the 12-month forecast reaching up to about 1.4 million dollars per month
- The 95 percent confidence interval on the ARIMA forecast is very narrow meaning high confidence in the numbers
- Prophet model also confirms the same linear upward trend with very tight prediction bands

TIME SERIES DECOMPOSITION FINDINGS

- The revenue series has a very strong upward trend component confirming consistent business growth
- There is a seasonal component present with fluctuations of roughly plus or minus 50,000 to 100,000 dollars
- Seasonal dips are seen around April and May each year based on the yearly component chart
- Seasonal peaks appear around March and November each year
- The residual component is small and random meaning the models capture most of the patterns well
- ACF analysis of the original series shows strong autocorrelation confirming the trend
- After differencing the series becomes stationary which validates using ARIMA for forecasting

SECTION 5 - CHURN PREDICTION MODEL PERFORMANCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

- Three machine learning models were trained to predict which customers will churn
- Models trained - Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and Gradient Boosting

MODEL ACCURACY RESULTS

- Logistic Regression achieved ROC AUC score of 0.995 which is near perfect
- Random Forest achieved ROC AUC score of 1.000 meaning perfect prediction on test data
- Gradient Boosting also achieved ROC AUC score of 1.000
- All three models achieved near perfect scores on Accuracy, Precision, Recall, and F1

CONFUSION MATRIX - RANDOM FOREST

- Out of 869 active customers in the test set the model correctly identified all 869 as active
- Out of 131 churned customers the model correctly identified all 131 as churned
- Zero false positives and zero false negatives meaning the model made no mistakes on test data
- Key Insight - The model can reliably predict in advance which customers are about to leave

TOP FEATURES FOR CHURN PREDICTION

- Recency Days meaning how many days since a customer last had activity is the most important feature with an importance score of about 0.51
- This is the single most powerful predictor of churn by far
- Lifetime Months meaning how long the customer has been with the company is the second most important at about 0.17
- Transaction Count meaning how many purchases the customer has made is third at about 0.12
- Customer Age in Days is fourth at about 0.12
- Tickets Per Month and Usage Score are smaller but still meaningful features
- Key Insight - If a customer has not been active recently they are very likely to churn soon

SECTION 6 - RISK STRATIFICATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

- The churn model was used to assign each customer a churn probability score
- Customers were then grouped into risk categories based on these scores

RISK CATEGORY BREAKDOWN

- Low Risk customers meaning churn probability below 50 percent - 2,663 customers which is 80.2 percent of total
- Very High Risk customers meaning churn probability above 50 percent - 650 customers which is 19.6 percent of total
- Medium Risk customers - only 4 customers which is 0.1 percent
- High Risk customers - only 2 customers which is 0.1 percent

FINANCIAL RISK FROM AT-RISK CUSTOMERS

- At-Risk customers are defined as those with more than 50 percent churn probability - total of 652 customers
- The MRR tied to at-risk customers is 166,748 dollars per month
- If all at-risk customers were to leave the potential annual revenue loss would be 2,000,976 dollars
- The Low Risk group holds 740,587 dollars in MRR which is the safe and stable revenue base
- Key Insight - Retaining even half of the at-risk customers would save approximately 1 million dollars per year

SECTION 7 - COHORT RETENTION ANALYSIS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

- Cohort analysis tracks how groups of customers who joined in the same month behave over time

RETENTION RATE FINDINGS

- In the first month after joining nearly all cohorts retain 100 percent of their customers
- Retention starts dropping gradually from month 4 or 5 onwards
- By month 12 most cohorts still retain around 91 to 96 percent of their original customers
- The 2020-01 cohort which is the oldest has maintained around 80 to 82 percent retention even after 50 plus months
- More recent cohorts like 2021-07 and 2022-01 show higher retention curves in their early months
- Key Insight - The product has strong early retention and most customer loss happens gradually over many months rather than in sudden drops

REVENUE PER CUSTOMER OVER TIME

- Average revenue per customer starts at around 290 dollars in the first month
- It stabilizes at around 265 to 270 dollars from month 2 onwards
- Revenue per customer stays very consistent even after 50 or more months showing high product stickiness
- Monthly cohort sizes (new customers per month) are relatively stable at around 75 to 95 new customers per month

SECTION 10 - KEY TAKEAWAYS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WHAT IS GOING WELL

- Revenue growth is very strong and consistent from 2020 to 2024 with no major dips
- Churn prediction models are extremely accurate with near perfect scores
- Customer retention rates are high especially in early months after joining
- CLV to CAC ratio of 14.33 is excellent meaning customers bring in far more than they cost to acquire
- Payback period of only 5 months means the business recovers acquisition costs quickly
- Revenue forecasting is highly accurate at 0.73 percent error showing the business is predictable

AREAS THAT NEED ATTENTION

- 652 customers are at very high risk of churning representing 166,748 dollars in monthly revenue at risk
- The At Risk RFM segment is the largest segment at 794 customers and also generates the most revenue
- Finance and Technology industries have the highest churn rates and should be targeted for retention efforts
- Over 18 percent of all customers fall in the At Risk RFM category which is very high
- The Enterprise segment despite being the premium tier generates the lowest total revenue due to small customer count
- USA dependence in geography means the business is not well diversified geographically

ACTIONABLE STEPS

- Use the churn prediction model in production to flag at-risk customers before they leave
- Focus retention campaigns on the At Risk RFM segment especially high CLV customers
- Offer longer contracts with discounts to reduce churn since data shows longer contracts have lower churn
- Monitor usage scores closely since low usage is a strong predictor of upcoming churn
- Target Finance and Technology industry customers with dedicated customer success efforts
- Consider expanding marketing efforts outside USA to reduce geographical concentration risk
